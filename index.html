Warm Visitors & Warm Accounts

Foundations·4 lessons · 22 minBeginner

Warmly starts with one question: who is actually on your website right now? This course covers the two answers, how our best customers turn them into revenue, and how the data gets into Warmly in the first place.

▶ Lesson 1
5:12
Warm Visitors vs Warm Accounts
Lesson videoPlaceholder · coming soon

Warm Visitors vs Warm Accounts

Beginner5:12Mark complete

Two layers of identity, one website:

  • Warm Visitors are person-level. Warmly de-anonymizes individual visitors and resolves name, title, company, LinkedIn, and email where available, so you can reach a specific human.
  • Warm Accounts are company-level. When we can identify the company but not a named person, that traffic still surfaces as a Warm Account so you never miss a buying team researching you.

Out of the box most of your traffic is anonymous to you. Warmly's job is to convert as much of it as possible into named people and known companies you can act on.

Nuance

Identification rates vary by traffic mix. US B2B traffic resolves at the highest rates; consumer, mobile, and EU traffic resolve lower for privacy and network reasons. Judge Warmly on the accounts it surfaces, not on a single visitor.

▶ Lesson 2
6:01
How top customers leverage it
Lesson videoPlaceholder · coming soon

How our best customers leverage it

Beginner6:01Mark complete

The teams that get the most out of Warmly do four things consistently:

  • Real-time owner alerts. When a target or high-intent account lands, the account owner is pinged in Slack with full context, while the visit is still live.
  • Prioritized follow-up. Reps work accounts showing live interest first, instead of grinding a cold list top to bottom.
  • Agent triggers. Identified visits fire the Inbound Agent (chat) and Outbound Agent (email / sequence) automatically.
  • ABM measurement. They see which target accounts actually visit and which campaigns drive them.
Pro move

Top performers route on the account, not the page. A VP of Sales from a target account sitting on your careers page is still a buying signal worth a play.

▶ Lesson 3

Lesson videoPlaceholder · coming soon

How you leverage it

Beginner5:30Mark complete

The whole platform follows one loop: Identify → Segment → Act → Measure.

  • Segment to define who matters (course 301).
  • Act by attaching segments to the Inbound Agent (chat) and Outbound Agent (email), and alerting the right owner.
  • Measure in reports, then refine the segment and the message.
Tip

Don't try to action every visitor on day one. Start with one tight segment (ICP on a high-intent page) and one action (Slack alert to the owner). Expand once it's working.

How you get this data

Beginner5:08Mark complete

Prospecting vs inbound
Lesson videoPlaceholder · coming soon

Prospecting vs inbound, the nuance

Beginner4:10Mark complete
  • Inbound means people already on your site. You react to demonstrated interest and lean on first-party website signals.
  • Prospecting / outbound means net-new accounts that match your ICP and may never have visited. You go find them, leaning on firmographic, technographic, and topic-intent data to build target lists.
  • They use different signals, different plays, and different messaging.
Nuance

▶ Lesson 3
4:55
Signal nuances & licensing
Lesson videoPlaceholder · coming soon

Signal nuances & licensing

Beginner4:55Mark complete

Not every signal is yours to use just because Warmly can display it. Some are licensed data tied to your own entitlements.

Important · G2

The G2 buyer-intent signal requires an active G2 subscription on your side. Warmly can surface it, but without your own G2 entitlement the signal will not populate. The same plan-dependent rule applies to some other third-party intent providers.

  • First-party website signals are always available once your script tag is live, no extra licensing.
  • Third-party intent (G2, topic data) depends on your subscription with that provider and your Warmly plan.
Tip

Before you build a play that depends on a third-party signal, confirm with your FDE or CSM that it's enabled on your plan, so you're not orchestrating off a signal that never fires.

Setting up a segment properly
Lesson videoPlaceholder · coming soon

How to set up a segment properly

Intermediate6:00Mark complete
  • 1. Name it clearly. You'll reuse it across agents and plays, so "ICP · Pricing page · 7d" beats "Segment 4".
  • 2. Add your filters from the dimensions in lesson 1.
  • 3. Choose ALL vs ANY logic (next lesson) so the conditions combine the way you intend.
  • 4. Add inclusion and exclusion rules to keep the wrong people out.
  • 5. Preview the live audience before you save, sanity-check the size and a few example accounts.
  • 6. Save and attach it to an agent, alert, or play.
Tip

If the preview audience is huge, your logic is probably too loose (likely an ANY that should be ALL). If it's empty, it's too tight, or a filter depends on a signal you don't have, like G2 without a subscription.

▶ Lesson 3
5:05
ALL vs ANY
Lesson videoPlaceholder · coming soon

The nuance of ALL vs ANY

Intermediate5:05Mark complete

This one trips up almost everyone. It's the difference between AND and OR:

LogicMeansEffect on audience
ALL ANDEvery condition must be trueNarrows, smaller and more precise
ANY ORAt least one condition is trueWidens, larger and looser

Example. "Industry = SaaS" + "Viewed pricing" + "Employees > 200":

  • With ALL: only SaaS companies over 200 employees that viewed pricing. Tight.
  • With ANY: anyone who is SaaS, or viewed pricing, or is over 200 employees. Huge and unfocused.
Nuance

You can nest them: an ALL group that contains an ANY group. e.g. ALL of [is ICP] AND [viewed pricing OR viewed demo]. That's how you get precise without being brittle.

▶ Lesson 4
5:25
Inclusion & exclusion
Lesson videoPlaceholder · coming soon

Inclusion & exclusion, and why it matters

Intermediate5:25Mark complete
  • Inclusion keeps the audience to who you want: target industries, sizes, regions, seniorities.
  • Exclusion keeps the wrong people out: existing customers, closed-lost, competitors, internal/employee traffic, and accounts with an open deal you don't want a play to disturb.
Why it matters

Exclusion is what stops you emailing a paying customer a "new prospect" pitch, or letting an outbound play step on a deal your AE is already closing. It protects the relationship and keeps your reports clean. Always exclude current customers from acquisition plays.

Connects to

The "if no company / if no deal exists" routing rules in the Troubleshooting Clinic are exclusion logic in disguise. Same idea, applied to routing.

501

Troubleshooting Clinic

Operate·8 common fixesAll levels

The issues that trip teams up most, each with the symptom and the exact fix. Click to expand. If you're stuck after this, your FDE is one message away.

01 The script tag isn't installed (or only on some pages)+
Symptom: no visitors or accounts appear, or data is patchy across pages. Fix
Confirm the Warmly snippet loads on every page, including subdomains and landing pages. If you use Google Tag Manager, check the tag is published and firing on All Pages. Load a page, open dev tools, and verify the Warmly script request succeeds. No tag, no data, this is the number-one root cause.
02 Warm Offers, multiple packages+
Symptom: an offer won't show even though it looks configured. Fix
If your account has multiple packages, an offer attached to one package won't appear on a page loading a different package. Confirm the offer lives on the same package your site is actually loading, then republish.
03 Warm Offers, forgot to toggle it on+
Symptom: offer built, segment correct, still nothing. Fix
The offer is saved but not enabled. Open the offer and flip the toggle on, then publish. It sounds obvious, it's also one of the most common tickets.
04 Segment inclusion, "if no company / if no deal" logic+
Symptom: the wrong people enter a play, or known accounts get skipped. Fix
Build the guard rails explicitly: if no company exists, then… and if no deal exists, then…. These conditional inclusion rules decide what happens to unmatched traffic. Without them, anonymous or company-less visits can fall into a play you meant only for known accounts. See course 301, lesson 4.
05 Routing, the user must exist in your CRM / MAP+
Symptom: a visit doesn't route to the right owner. Fix
Owner-based routing depends on the user existing in your CRM or MAP. If the account or contact isn't there, Warmly has no owner to route to. Make sure the record (and its owner) exists in HubSpot / Salesforce, or set a fallback route.
06 Testing chat / features, cache & incognito+
Symptom: "I changed the chat but I still see the old one." Fix
Your browser is caching the old experience. Always test in an incognito / private window or a fresh browser so you see the current build, not a cached one. Clear cache if testing repeatedly from the same browser.
07 Chat ordering, surfacing the right chat to the right ICP+
Symptom: the wrong chat experience shows for a visitor. Fix
When multiple chats can match a visitor, order matters, the first matching experience wins. Put your most specific, highest-priority ICP chats above broader catch-all chats so the right audience gets the right conversation.
08 Warm Visitors / Warm Accounts filtered-audience display bug+
Symptom: the filtered audience in Warm Visitors / Warm Accounts doesn't display correctly even though your orchestrations fire as expected. Known issue
This is a known display bug, the underlying orchestration is still correct, so your plays run on the right audience even when the filtered view looks wrong. A fix is in progress. If it's blocking you, flag it to your FDE so we can prioritize.
